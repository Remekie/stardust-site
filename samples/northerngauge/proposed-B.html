Technical Position

Why Northern Gauge uses
solid ring design over AGD

Northern Gauge manufactures solid ring gauges rather than AGD-style adjustable ring gauges. This is a deliberate technical choice, not a cost decision.

In a solid ring gauge, the pitch diameter is physically measurable — you can verify it directly with a calibrated instrument. In AGD adjustable designs, the pitch diameter is set by a setting plug that may itself be worn or out of tolerance. You cannot measure an adjustable ring without the set plug.

Solid rings are also drop-resistant: the ring doesn't go out of adjustment if it falls on the shop floor. An AGD ring that falls must be re-set and re-verified before use.

Reference: ASME B47.1 — Gage Blanks. Northern Gauge NIST-traceable calibration certificates document measured pitch diameter on every solid ring gauge.

Northern Gauge

Solid Ring Gauge

  • Pitch diameter directly measurable
  • No set plug required for verification
  • Drop-resistant — stays in adjustment
  • NIST-traceable cert documents measured PD
  • Dimensional stability over full gauge life

Alternative

AGD Adjustable Ring

  • Pitch diameter set by plug, not measured
  • Requires set plug for every verification
  • Falls can shift adjustment — must re-verify
  • Cert documents setting plug, not ring itself
  • Backlash in adjustment mechanism over time
