Technical Position

Why solid ring
over AGD adjustable

Northern Gauge manufactures solid ring gauges — not AGD-style adjustable rings. This is a deliberate technical choice.

In a solid ring gauge, the pitch diameter is physically measurable. You can verify it directly with a calibrated instrument. In AGD adjustable designs, the pitch diameter is set by a setting plug that may itself carry error — and cannot be independently verified.

Solid rings are also drop-resistant. An AGD ring that falls on the shop floor must be re-set and re-verified before use. A solid ring that falls is unchanged.

Reference: ASME B47.1 — Gage Blanks. Northern Gauge NIST-traceable calibration certificates document measured pitch diameter on every solid ring gauge shipped.
